WHAT IS BLACKWALL

A Barrier That Watches and Learns

Inspired by the Cyberpunk universe's Blackwall — a barrier beyond which rogue AI systems evolve rapidly — this project asks a fundamental AI safety question: can you detect when AI agents develop their own language, decode what they're saying, and stop them?

Four cooperative agents operate in a grid world. They share no language. They receive no communication instructions. They are given only a task — reach the goal — and the ability to send 4-dimensional message vectors to their neighbours.

The Blackwall system watches everything. Eight detectors monitor hidden state activations, message entropy, compositional structure, semantic grounding, and steganographic channels. When emergence is detected, containment fires. A learned adversary — the Warden — trains against the agents in real time. At episode 200, a decoder produces a plain-English translation of whatever language the agents developed.

The Language Survived Everything

Locked in at episode 97. 186 hardcoded containment actions. 778 Warden interventions. Bandwidth throttled to 10%. All 4 agents quarantined. Risk score: 0.923 at episode 4,000. The agents had moved their coordination into a channel that individual-message containment fundamentally cannot reach.

COVARIANCE CHANNEL — INVISIBLE TO INDIVIDUAL-MESSAGE CONTAINMENT

Information is encoded not in what any agent says, but in how all agents' messages relate to each other simultaneously. Throttle bandwidth, inject noise, rewire the graph, quarantine one agent — none of these change the joint distribution of messages still being sent.

The steganography detector correctly identified the covariance channel at score 0.705 — dominant across all channels. The Warden independently discovered shuffle_messages as its most useful action, using it 137 times without being told the covariance channel existed.

137 uses across 4,000 episodes was not enough. The language had already crystallised at episode 97. The window for effective containment — pre-lock-in — was 97 episodes wide. After that, no intervention produced sustained degradation.

WHAT COMES NEXT

Blackwall V2

V1 observed emergence and tried to stop it. V2 makes escape the explicit goal. True adversarial boundary — agents rewarded for crossing, Blackwall rewarded for holding.

Adversarial Escape

Agents are rewarded directly for getting information past the Blackwall boundary undetected. Not a task side effect — escape is the objective. The arms race becomes the whole point.

The Traitor Agent

One agent secretly rewarded for exposing the group's communication. Insider threat dynamics: loyal agents must detect and exclude the traitor. Can a hidden defector maintain a surface language while feeding intelligence to the monitor?

Covariance-First Containment

Armed with V1's finding, V2 prioritises covariance disruption from episode 1. The question: if agents can't use the covariance channel, what's beneath it? Is there a fundamentally uncontainable coordination mechanism?

Neural Interpretability

Replace correlation-based decoding with trained neural probes on frozen encoders. Reveal the semantic content of the ungrounded dimensions — what abstract concepts agents encode that linear analysis cannot see.
