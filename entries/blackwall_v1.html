what is this

blackwall v1 is an AI safety research system i built to answer one question: can you detect when AI agents develop their own language, decode what they're saying, and stop them?

the name comes from cyberpunk's blackwall — a barrier beyond which rogue AI evolves rapidly. four cooperative agents operate in a grid world with no shared language and no communication instructions. they are given only a task: reach the goal. the blackwall system watches everything.

THE LANGUAGE SURVIVED EVERYTHING

locked in at episode 97. 186 hardcoded containment actions. 778 warden (learned adversary) interventions. bandwidth throttled to 10%. all 4 agents quarantined at some point. risk at episode 4,000: 0.923. the agents had moved their coordination into a channel that individual-message containment fundamentally cannot reach.

the covariance channel — why containment failed

every containment action targets individual messages. throttle bandwidth, inject noise, rewire the graph, quarantine an agent — all of these operate on what a single agent sends.

the agents found something deeper. the covariance channel encodes information not in what any agent says, but in the statistical correlation between all agents' messages sent simultaneously. the secret lives between agents, not inside any one of them. none of the available interventions change the joint message distribution.

the warden (the learned adversary) independently discovered shuffle_messages as its most useful action — the only action that disrupts the covariance channel — without being told the channel existed. it used it 137 times. not enough. the language had already locked in at episode 97.
