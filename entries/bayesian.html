bayesian counterfactual early-warning system for icu deterioration

mimic-iv · gru-d · uncertainty estimation · counterfactual simulation · clinical time series

1. why i worked on this

in icu settings, patient deterioration rarely happens instantly. it usually develops through subtle, time-evolving changes in vital signs and laboratory measurements. however, most early-warning systems still rely on fixed thresholds applied to static snapshots of patient data.

while studying this space, i realized three major problems:

  • most systems ignore temporal patient trajectories
  • missing values are treated as noise instead of informative signals
  • risk scores are deterministic and do not communicate uncertainty

in a high-stakes environment like the icu, giving a number without explaining how confident the model is felt incomplete. this project started from that discomfort.

2. project overview

instead of treating icu risk prediction as a static classification task, i framed it as a continuous, time-dependent monitoring problem.

the system integrates:

  • temporal modeling of irregular icu time series
  • explicit handling of informative missingness
  • bayesian uncertainty estimation
  • model-implied counterfactual risk simulation

rather than outputting a single alert score, the model produces:

  • a dynamic risk trajectory over time
  • time-varying predictive uncertainty
  • counterfactual trajectories under hypothetical early intervention

the goal was not just prediction accuracy, but structured decision support.

3. technical approach

data representation

icu time series are irregular and asynchronously sampled. instead of naive imputation, each time step was represented as:

  • observed value (xₜ,d)
  • observation mask (mₜ,d)
  • time since last measurement (Δtₜ,d)

this allows the model to distinguish between true physiological stability and missing data due to clinical decision-making.

missingness-aware modeling (gru-d)

the model is built on gru-d, which introduces learnable decay mechanisms for stale variables and hidden states. instead of filling missing values blindly, the network learns how information fades over time.

uncertainty estimation

to quantify epistemic uncertainty, monte carlo dropout was retained during inference. multiple stochastic forward passes generate:

  • mean predicted risk trajectory
  • time-dependent predictive variance

uncertainty increases during sparse or unstable periods, which aligns well with intuitive clinical reasoning.

counterfactual simulation

beyond prediction, i implemented model-implied counterfactual analysis. after selecting an intervention time t₀, trajectories are perturbed to simulate early stabilization. the model then generates both observed and counterfactual risk paths.

this is not a fully causal framework, but it enables structured “what-if” exploration within the learned model space.

4. experimental setup

  • dataset: mimic-iv demo icu cohort
  • patient-level train / validation / test split
  • temporal hold-out evaluation
  • z-score normalization using training statistics only
  • gradient clipping for stability
  • evaluation beyond accuracy (calibration + uncertainty alignment)

5. key results

predictive performance

  • auroc: 0.955
  • auprc: 0.845
  • brier score: 0.149

performance remained strong despite dataset limitations, while calibration and uncertainty alignment remained stable.

behavioral findings

  • risk trajectories evolved smoothly over time
  • uncertainty increased during sparse periods
  • strong alignment between uncertainty and absolute error
  • counterfactual simulations reduced predicted risk under stabilization

7. limitations

  • evaluation limited to mimic-iv demo dataset
  • counterfactuals are model-implied, not causally identified
  • monte carlo dropout provides approximate bayesian inference
  • limited benchmarking against all possible baselines

8. future directions

  • validation on full-scale icu cohorts
  • explicit treatment modeling and causal extensions
  • reinforcement learning for sequential decision optimization
  • real-time deployment with human-in-the-loop evaluation
  • robust uncertainty calibration under distribution shift

  • gru-d implementation and optimization
  • bayesian uncertainty integration
  • counterfactual simulation framework
  • evaluation and manuscript structuring

overall, this project reflects my interest in combining deep learning, probabilistic modeling, and structured decision-support systems, especially in high-stakes environments.
