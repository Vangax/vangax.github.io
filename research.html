building tars: a short timeline

february 2025
project

the idea becomes real

for a long time, ever since i watched the movie interstellar, the idea of tars lived only in my head. in february 2025, i decided to stop thinking and actually try building something. i wanted a small assistant that worked on my own machine and felt personal.

i installed a large language model locally using ollama. keeping everything offline was intentional so i could understand how an ai system behaves without shortcuts.

late february 2025
system design

giving the system a personality

once the system was stable, i focused on behavior. i added adjustable settings for humor, joke frequency, and seriousness to control how the system responds during conversations.

i added a trigger phrase, "hey its serious now tars", which switches the assistant into a focused mode where it only concentrates on solving the task.

learning how humans talk to machines

typing began to feel limiting, so i integrated a speech recognition pipeline and started interacting with tars using my voice.

i tuned the voice to stay calm and controlled. in non focused mode, tars would sometimes make jokes, which made the system feel human and enjoyable to use.

march 2025
research direction

from experiment to research interest

by march 2025, this was no longer just an experiment. using the system daily changed how i see autonomous ai systems as tools that can help humans when connectivity is unreliable.

this made me want to explore these systems at a research level, focusing on control, reliability, and real-world usefulness.

my interest in discovery systems focuses on using anomaly detection and data-driven methods to surface rare or unexpected patterns in complex datasets.

i am especially interested in how these systems can assist scientific discovery by guiding human attention rather than replacing human judgment.

i am deeply interested in applying machine learning and deep learning to astrophysical data, especially where noise and scale make traditional analysis difficult.
