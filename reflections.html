on research

on engram

engram was never something i planned it just came into my mind randomly one night while i was thinking and it hit really deep like not a normal idea it felt real and heavy. i was thinking about people who are going to be left behind in this fast moving world everything is speeding up ai tech everything and at the same time people are getting lonelier day by day. and i was reading bhagwadgita around that time and one idea stuck with me that once you find your true self you stop feeling suffering the same way you start observing it from a third perspective almost like supporting your own material body instead of being controlled by it that is where engram started forming in my head like what if there was something that reminds you of who you actually are but in a safe way not something addictive not something that replaces you but something that brings you back to yourself again and again i started very raw just testing llms writing code manually no proper structure just trying things out taking some ui inspiration from cyberpunk because i wanted it to feel a certain way something minimal but deep something that doesnt feel like a normal app. after that i started testing the llm with constraints like identity local memory soul identity all these ideas that i didnt even fully understand at that time but i kept experimenting then i moved to ui and that phase was honestly full of failures things breaking layouts not making sense nothing feeling right but i kept going. then i shifted towards features initially i had like 19 features in mind all felt important at that moment but as i started building i realised most of them were unnecessary so i cut it down to just 2 core things and focused only on them i was working almost 18 hours a day which was honestly very unhealthy but at that time i felt like i was getting closer to something real like this could actually become something people use something that helps them so i kept pushing. but then the biggest slowdown came from hallucination problems with the llm and not finding a proper solution especially because i wanted to keep everything local which made it way harder due to limited context size and constraints it felt like i was fighting the system itself at that point still i didnt stop i kept trying different approaches i built an 8 layer recall system tried to structure memory better worked on something like a soul system to maintain continuity of identity i even experimented with something like a memory palace wing architecture released in first week of april which sounded good in theory but didnt really work out practically right now im still working on it just at a slower pace along with other things its not as chaotic as before but the idea is still there it never left it still feels like something important something that needs to exist i just need to figure out how to make it real in the right way.

"i was thinking of something way ahead for current time"

transitioning

what just happened?

i was studying physics and preparing for medical with applied maths during my senior secondary and at that time i was fully into it like that was the only path i could see for myself. i used to spend hours just studying concepts, solving problems, thinking about how everything connects. i was really invested in it not just academically but mentally as well then in 2024 during my school farewell there was this one moment that stayed with me more than anything else. it was very emotional like everything was ending at once. after the whole ceremony when everyone had started leaving me and my bestfriend just stayed back on the ground we didnt really want to leave so we just picked up the ball and started playing football in our formal clothes, pants, shirts everything. we played for more than an hour just talking in between, playing, laughing but also feeling that heaviness that this is the last time we are here like this it wasnt even about the game, it was about holding on to that moment for a little longer. we both knew things were about to change and we just didnt want it to end that quickly. i never really thought i would miss school this much but that day made me realise it but things didnt go the way i expected. i couldnt get into medical and that whole phase kind of hit me harder than i thought it would because it wasnt just about not getting in. it felt like the direction i had in my head just disappeared and during that time i also slowly phased out of physics without even realising it properly. it was like everything just paused and i didnt know what to hold on to then i ended up coming here for college at symbiosis mostly because someone told us its a nice place and at that point i wasnt overthinking too much. i just needed to move forward with something instead of staying stuck. but even after coming here there was this thought in the back of my mind like what if i had continued with physics, what if i had taken that route seriously but over time i started understanding that im not actually limited by the system or by the degree im doing right now. just because im studying programming doesnt mean i have to leave physics behind. i can still go as deep as i want into it on my own terms and at the same time apply that way of thinking into what im building with ai and systems my first year was pretty simple mostly learning the basics of programming, understanding things from scratch, playing football whenever i could and just enjoying small things like good food haha. but even in that phase there was a quiet shift happening in how i was thinking towards the end of 2024 i started getting back into things on my own not in a structured way, just exploring, researching whatever felt interesting. it was kind of self driven and a bit isolated i wasnt really showing it to people i was just going with the flow trying to understand things in my own way again and slowly that curiosity started coming back then in 2025 i completed my internship at AIT GLOBAL and that experience changed a lot for me. it exposed me to real systems, real workflows, real problems and at the same time mr ajeet singh, the delivery head there helped me a lot in understanding things better, how systems actually work and how to think more practically not just theoretically that whole experience made me realise that i dont just want to sit and think anymore i actually want to build things, create things and apply what i know after that i entered my second year with a completely different mindset. there was a fire in me to build, to experiment, to push forward and at the same time keep going deeper into my own research but now with action not just thought.

i've learned to adapt finally!
